Newsletters: How do you build a scalable system for multiple markets?

Keywords UX Strategy, UX/UI
Role Design Lead
Industry Retail
Platforms Email, Salesforce

Project Description

As the Design Lead on this project, I managed a small design team and oversaw the entire creative process from roadmap creation and strategic planning to design handover and feedback sessions. My role included collaborating closely with stakeholders, conducting interviews and workshops, and ensuring our process was aligned with each marketâ€™s unique needs while maintaining global brand consistency.

Problem
  • Design Inconsistencies: Different countries had been using distinct visual styles and design systems, leading to fragmentation across newsletters.
  • Divergent Stakeholder Needs: Each market maintained its own strategy and user expectations, resulting in a lack of uniformity.
  • High Operational Costs: Weekly production was costly due to reliance on external agencies and a complex process for creating graphic materials.
  • Insufficient Engagement: Newsletters were underperforming in terms of profit, with low personalization, excessive content, and high bounce rates.

Process

Desk Research
  • User Tracking and Analytics: We analyzed usage data to identify which elements were most engaging or notably missing.
  • Market Research: We reviewed existing country-specific analysis files to understand prior findings and leverage available data.
  • Email Analysis: By gathering and reviewing all sent newsletters, we identified display issues, inconsistencies, and variances in strategy across markets.
Discovery
  • Country Workshops: Seven workshops were conducted with local marketing teams.
  • User Interviews: In-depth interviews with eight newsletter users captured their expectations and pain points.
Definition

We defined two primary user segments:

  • Loyal Customers: Regular readers who closely follow daily newsletter content.
  • Contest Hunters: Users primarily interested in exclusive offers or contests.

Ideation

  • Adjusting Existing Design System: Allowed independence, but could result in inconsistency and higher costs.
  • Fixed Template per Country: Lowered costs but lacked flexibility.
  • Modular Content Block Approach (Chosen): Enabled flexibility and consistency across markets using pre-tested content blocks.

Design

Wireframes

Designed mobile-first, with a focus on clarity and modularity. Received feedback from local teams to validate direction.

Mockups & Iterations

Feedback from ~50 suggestions led to categorized improvements. This maintained scalability and local adaptability.

Final Outcome

  • Unified Design Consistency: Cohesive brand across regions.
  • Increased Operational Efficiency: Less external agency reliance.
  • Enhanced User Engagement: Improved click-throughs and personalization.
  • Data-Driven Iterations: Ongoing optimization through analytics.

Summary

A modular, scalable newsletter system adaptable for each market. The redesign enhanced brand consistency, reduced creation effort, and laid a strong foundation for future growth tied to personalization and loyalty programs.
